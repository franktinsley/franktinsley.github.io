SDF modeling engine

SWIFT + METAL · ~50,000 LINES · ~650 COMMITS · iOS / iPadOS / macOS · 2026

A real-time signed-distance-field modeling engine and app, built solo in about four months: ray-marched implicit surfaces, smooth booleans and organic blends, procedural geometry, and marching-cubes export to STL/OBJ. I wrote the full Metal stack — compute, fragment, and vertex — and tuned it to hold interactive frame rates on phone GPUs, not just desktops. The renderer behind this page is its little sibling. Konami System 573, reimplemented on an FPGA

~22,000 LINES CUSTOM RTL · ~1 MONTH · MiSTer / DE10-NANO · VERIFIED FRAME-ACCURATE VS MAME ON HARDWARE

A re-implementation of Konami's System 573 — the PlayStation-derived arcade board behind Dance Dance Revolution — as a MiSTer FPGA core. Roughly 22,000 lines of custom RTL in about a month, through an AI-accelerated workflow built around automated verification: changes proven frame-accurate against the MAME reference emulator by frame-diff tooling running against the real FPGA. Digital design is a different discipline from shading; the transferable part was making the verification loop fast enough to iterate like software.

03 — COLOPHON: HOW THIS PAGE IS DRAWN

The background isn't a video or a texture. It's a scene — and this panel you're reading it on is one of its objects.

  • One scene, two passes. Everything behind the text — panels and lamps — is a single ray-marched signed-distance scene, evaluated in one WebGPU fragment pass. A second small pass scales it to your screen, tonemaps, and dithers. That's the entire pipeline: no textures, no bloom, no post-effect chain.
  • Parametric materials. Clear and frosted glass are one shading path with a continuously blended frost parameter; chrome and gold share one metal path. The blend weight rides the same smooth-minimum that merges the geometry, so materials transition across blend necks without seams.
  • Honest refraction. Clear glass earns one refracted bounce per pixel: a march through its own interior, bisection-refined so the exit lands on the surface, then a single capped march out to whatever actually sits behind — total internal reflection included. Reflection rays never re-march; they sample an analytic environment. That's the performance contract, stated exactly.
  • The periphery is the cheap surface. Past the frost threshold, the refracted bounce switches itself off and the glass becomes a pure diffuser. Every panel you're not reading rests past that threshold, so the expensive clear-glass path only ever runs on the one panel in focus — the depth-of-field hierarchy and the performance optimization are the same rule.
  • Analytic lamplight. The halo around each lamp is a closed-form line integral evaluated along the ray — true volumetric falloff with zero bloom passes. Frosted glass reuses the same integral with a widened spread: the frost is the diffuser.
  • HTML first. Every word on this page is plain HTML; the canvas sits behind it and never draws a letter. The DOM owns layout — the glass is re-fitted to the text's real geometry, so zoom, font scaling, find-in-page, and screen readers just work.
  • A disciplined budget. 60 fps target on Apple Silicon, adaptive resolution elsewhere — the readout below is measured live, not asserted, and it only prints numbers the page can actually measure. When you stop scrolling, rendering stops entirely; the readout says so.
